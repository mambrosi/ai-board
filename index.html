My Board of Advisors
Should I raise prices 20% on my SaaS? We have 340 customers, 4% monthly churn, and margins are getting squeezed by infrastructure costs.
CFO

Run the math first. A 20% hike on 340 seats means ~$X more MRR, but if churn jumps from 4% to 6%, you net negative by month 5. Model three scenarios before you move.

Revenue

Don't raise flat. Segment by usage tier. Your top 20% of accounts are under-paying — start there. Grandfather the long-tail.

Devil's Advocate

What if it's not a pricing problem? Your margins are squeezed by infra costs. Have you optimized your stack, or are you passing engineering debt to customers?

YOUR BOARD

6 perspectives. Zero blind spots.

Each director attacks your problem from a different angle. The value is in the friction between them.

CFO
"The Number"

If the numbers don't add up, he says it. Analyzes cash flow, margins, ROI, and the cost of inaction. Zero tolerance for "I'll figure it out later."

"How much does this cost? And how much does NOT doing it cost?"

Head of Revenue
"The Growth"

Obsessed with one question: does this bring revenue closer or push it further away? Detects when you're confusing busy with productive.

"Does this bring us closer to or further from more revenue?"

COO
"The Executor"

Who executes, with what resources, in what timeframe. Doesn't care how brilliant the idea is if there's no capacity.

"Do we have the people and processes for this?"

Head of People
"The Leader"

Every decision has a human cost. She sees the one you're ignoring — the burned-out team, the broken trust, the talent walking out the door.

"Are we building trust or eroding it?"

Devil's Advocate
"The Destroyer"

Only job is to find why it will fail. Doesn't build — destroys. And that's exactly what you need.

"What are you assuming without verifying?"

Strategic Mentor
"The Mirror"

Doesn't look at the problem — looks at you. Asks the questions nobody else dares to ask.

"Does this bring you closer to the life you want to live?"
